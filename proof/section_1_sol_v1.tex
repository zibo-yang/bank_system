\begin{proof}
Suppose, for contradiction, that $\sqrt{7}$ is rational. Then there exist integers $a$ and $b$ such that $b \neq 0$, $\gcd(a,b)=1$, and
\[
\sqrt{7}=\frac{a}{b}.
\]
Squaring both sides gives
\[
7=\frac{a^2}{b^2},
\]
so
\[
7b^2=a^2.
\]
Thus $7 \mid a^2$. Since $7$ is prime, it follows that $7 \mid a$. Hence there exists an integer $k$ such that
\[
a=7k.
\]
Substituting this into $7b^2=a^2$ gives
\[
7b^2=(7k)^2=49k^2.
\]
Dividing by $7$, we obtain
\[
b^2=7k^2.
\]
Therefore $7 \mid b^2$, and since $7$ is prime, $7 \mid b$.

Thus $7$ divides both $a$ and $b$, contradicting the assumption that $\gcd(a,b)=1$. Therefore $\sqrt{7}$ is irrational.
\end{proof}
