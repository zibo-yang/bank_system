**Verdict: Not fully valid as written, but the structure is very close and is repairable.**  
If the stated lemmas are proved with the dependency issues fixed, they would indeed imply Conjecture 3.

## Issues

### 1. Subproblem 2 appears to depend on Subproblem 3, but comes before it

Subproblem 2 tries to prove that the identity scaling minimizes  
\[
\|\operatorname{diag}(1,\delta)H\operatorname{diag}(1,1/\delta)\|_p.
\]

The proposed approach compares lower bounds for \(\delta\neq 1\) against the value \(2^{1-1/p}\), which is exactly the formula later proved in Subproblem 3:
\[
\|H\|_p=2^{1-1/p}.
\]

So as written, Subproblem 2 either implicitly uses Subproblem 3 or needs to contain its own proof of the relevant unscaled norm value. This is a dependency/order problem.

**Fix:** Either reorder the proof so that Subproblem 3 comes before Subproblem 2, or make Subproblem 2 self-contained by proving the needed value of \(\|H\|_p\) inside it.

---

### 2. The reduction to one scaling parameter in Subproblem 2 should be stated explicitly

For a general positive diagonal scaling of \(H\),
\[
\Delta=\operatorname{diag}(a,b),
\]
one obtains
\[
\Delta H\Delta^{-1}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & a/b\\
b/a & -1
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

Subproblem 2 only considers scalings of the form
\[
\operatorname{diag}(1,\delta)H\operatorname{diag}(1,1/\delta).
\]

This is fine because multiplying \(\Delta\) by a positive scalar does not change \(\Delta H\Delta^{-1}\), so only the ratio \(b/a\) matters. But that normalization should be explicitly stated. Otherwise Subproblem 2 appears to treat only a subset of admissible scalings.

---

### 3. Subproblem 4 uses \(\rho_k(I_{n-2})=1\) without listing it as a lemma

In the synthesis one uses
\[
\rho_k(H\oplus I_{n-2})
=
\max(\rho_k(H),\rho_k(I_{n-2}))
\]
and then substitutes
\[
\rho_k(I_{n-2})=1.
\]

This fact is elementary, since diagonal scalings commute with the identity, but it is still an unlisted dependency. It should either be included as a short lemma or proved inside Subproblem 4.

---

### 4. Subproblem 2 proves more than is needed

Subproblem 2 claims the infimum is **uniquely** achieved at \(\delta=1\). For the final theorem, uniqueness is unnecessary. One only needs
\[
\rho_p(H)=\|H\|_p.
\]

This is not a logical flaw, but it is a small relevance issue: the uniqueness part is stronger than required.

---

## Completeness check

Modulo the issues above, the decomposition is complete.

Once one has:

1. block-diagonal decoupling,
2. \(\rho_k(H)=\|H\|_k\),
3. \(\|H\|_k=2^{1-1/k}\),
4. \(\rho_k(I_{n-2})=1\),

then for
\[
M=H\oplus I_{n-2}
\]
one gets
\[
\rho_k(M)
=
\max(2^{1-1/k},1)
=
2^{1-1/k}
\]
for all \(k\ge 2\). Therefore, for every integer \(p>2\),
\[
\rho_p(M)=2^{1-1/p}
<
2^{1-1/(p+1)}
=
\rho_{p+1}(M).
\]

Since \(n>6\) implies \(n-2\ge 5\), the block construction produces an \(n\times n\) real matrix for every required dimension. Thus there are no missing dimension or parity cases.

## Final assessment

The proof plan is **structurally sound in its main idea**, but **not fully valid as written** because of a dependency/order issue between Subproblems 2 and 3 and a missing explicit treatment of the identity block and scaling normalization. After those small repairs, the outline would form a complete proof structure for Conjecture 3.
