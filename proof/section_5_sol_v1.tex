\section{Embedding into Larger Dimensions}

Let $r \geq 3$ be an integer. For a real $k \times k$ matrix $A$, write
\[
\|A\|_{r \to r}
=
\sup_{x \neq 0} \frac{\|Ax\|_r}{\|x\|_r}
\]
for the induced $\ell^r$ operator norm, and define
\[
\rho_r(A)
=
\inf_{D \in \mathcal{D}_k^+}
\|DAD^{-1}\|_{r \to r},
\]
where $\mathcal{D}_k^+$ denotes the set of all positive diagonal $k \times k$ matrices.

We prove that for every $n \geq 7$,
\[
\rho_r(M_n)=\rho_r(C),
\]
where
\[
M_n=
\begin{pmatrix}
C & 0\\
0 & 0_{n-7}
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

First observe that every positive diagonal $n \times n$ matrix $D$ decomposes compatibly with the block structure of $M_n$ as
\[
D=
\begin{pmatrix}
D_1 & 0\\
0 & D_2
\end{pmatrix},
\]
where $D_1 \in \mathcal{D}_7^+$ and $D_2 \in \mathcal{D}_{n-7}^+$. When $n=7$, the block $D_2$ is absent and the argument below is interpreted in the evident way.

Since $D$ is block diagonal, we have
\[
DM_nD^{-1}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
D_1 & 0\\
0 & D_2
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
C & 0\\
0 & 0_{n-7}
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
D_1^{-1} & 0\\
0 & D_2^{-1}
\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
D_1 C D_1^{-1} & 0\\
0 & 0_{n-7}
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
Thus it remains to prove that for every $7 \times 7$ matrix $A$),
\[
\left\|
\begin{pmatrix}
A & 0\\
0 & 0_{n-7}
\end{pmatrix}
\right\|_{r \to r}
=
\|A\|_{r \to r}.
\]

Let
\[
B=
\begin{pmatrix}
A & 0\\
0 & 0_{n-7}
\end{pmatrix}.
\]
For any vector $x \in \mathbb{R}^n$, write $x=(x_1,x_2)$ with $x_1 \in \mathbb{R}^7$ and $x_2 \in \mathbb{R}^{n-7}$. Then
\[
Bx=(Ax_1,0).
\]
Therefore
\[
\|Bx\|_r=\|Ax_1\|_r
\leq
\|A\|_{r \to r}\|x_1\|_r.
\]
Since
\[
\|x_1\|_r \leq \left(\|x_1\|_r^r+\|x_2\|_r^r\right)^{1/r}
=
\|x\|_r,
\]
we obtain
\[
\|Bx\|_r
\leq
\|A\|_{r \to r}\|x\|_r.
\]
Taking the supremum over all nonzero $x \in \mathbb{R}^n$ gives
\[
\|B\|_{r \to r} \leq \|A\|_{r \to r}.
\]

Conversely, for every nonzero $y \in \mathbb{R}^7$, define $x=(y,0) \in \mathbb{R}^n$. Then
\[
Bx=(Ay,0),
\]
so
\[
\frac{\|Bx\|_r}{\|x\|_r}
=
\frac{\|Ay\|_r}{\|y\|_r}.
\]
Taking the supremum over all nonzero $y \in \mathbb{R}^7$ gives
\[
\|B\|_{r \to r} \geq \|A\|_{r \to r}.
\]
Hence
\[
\left\|
\begin{pmatrix}
A & 0\\
0 & 0_{n-7}
\end{pmatrix}
\right\|_{r \to r}
=
\|A\|_{r \to r}.
\]

Applying this with
\[
A=D_1 C D_1^{-1},
\]
we get
\[
\|DM_nD^{-1}\|_{r \to r}
=
\|D_1 C D_1^{-1}\|_{r \to r}.
\]
Therefore
\[
\rho_r(M_n)
=
\inf_{D \in \mathcal{D}_n^+}
\|DM_nD^{-1}\|_{r \to r}.
\]
Using the block decomposition $D=\operatorname{diag}(D_1,D_2)$, this becomes
\[
\rho_r(M_n)
=
\inf_{\substack{D_1 \in \mathcal{D}_7^+\\ D_2 \in \mathcal{D}_{n-7}^+}}
\|D_1 C D_1^{-1}\|_{r \to r}.
\]
The expression inside the infimum is independent of $D_2$. Since $\mathcal{D}_{n-7}^+$ is nonempty, taking the infimum over $D_2$ has no effect, and hence
\[
\rho_r(M_n)
=
\inf_{D_1 \in \mathcal{D}_7^+}
\|D_1 C D_1^{-1}\|_{r \to r}.
\]
By definition, the right-hand side is precisely $\rho_r(C)$. Thus
\[
\boxed{\rho_r(M_n)=\rho_r(C)}.
\]

This proves the desired embedding result for every integer $n \geq 7$ and every integer $r \geq 3$.
