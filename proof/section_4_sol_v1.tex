\[
\Gamma_r=\left(\frac{7^r+6}{7}\right)^{1/r}.
\]

We prove the stronger fact that power means are strictly increasing in their order for positive, nonconstant data.

**Lemma.** Let \(x_1,\dots,x_n>0\), let \(w_1,\dots,w_n>0\) with \(\sum_{i=1}^n w_i=1\), and suppose that \(x_1,\dots,x_n\) are not all equal. For \(0<p<q\), define

\[
M_p=\left(\sum_{i=1}^n w_i x_i^p\right)^{1/p},
\qquad
M_q=\left(\sum_{i=1}^n w_i x_i^q\right)^{1/q}.
\]

Then

\[
M_p<M_q.
\]

**Proof of the lemma.** Set

\[
\alpha=\frac{p}{q}.
\]

Since \(0<p<q\), we have

\[
0<\alpha<1.
\]

Let

\[
y_i=x_i^q>0.
\]

Then

\[
x_i^p=x_i^{q\alpha}=y_i^\alpha.
\]

Therefore

\[
M_p^p=\sum_{i=1}^n w_i y_i^\alpha.
\]

Also,

\[
M_q^p
=
\left(\left(\sum_{i=1}^n w_i x_i^q\right)^{1/q}\right)^p
=
\left(\sum_{i=1}^n w_i y_i\right)^{p/q}
=
\left(\sum_{i=1}^n w_i y_i\right)^\alpha.
\]

Now consider the function

\[
f(t)=t^\alpha
\qquad (t>0).
\]

Its second derivative is

\[
f''(t)=\alpha(\alpha-1)t^{\alpha-2}<0,
\]

because \(0<\alpha<1\). Hence \(f\) is strictly concave on \((0,\infty)\). By strict Jensen’s inequality,

\[
\sum_{i=1}^n w_i f(y_i)
<
f\left(\sum_{i=1}^n w_i y_i\right),
\]

because the weights \(w_i\) are all positive and the numbers \(y_i\) are not all equal. Thus

\[
\sum_{i=1}^n w_i y_i^\alpha
<
\left(\sum_{i=1}^n w_i y_i\right)^\alpha.
\]

Equivalently,

\[
M_p^p<M_q^p.
\]

Since \(p>0\), the function \(t\mapsto t^{1/p}\) is strictly increasing on \((0,\infty)\), so

\[
M_p<M_q.
\]

This proves the lemma. \(\square\)

Now apply the lemma with

\[
n=7,
\qquad
w_1=\cdots=w_7=\frac17,
\]

and

\[
(x_1,\dots,x_7)=(7,1,1,1,1,1,1).
\]

These entries are positive and not all equal. For each integer \(r\ge 3\),

\[
M_r
=
\left(\frac17\left(7^r+1^r+1^r+1^r+1^r+1^r+1^r\right)\right)^{1/r}
=
\left(\frac{7^r+6}{7}\right)^{1/r}
=
\Gamma_r.
\]

Since \(r<r+1\), the strict power-mean monotonicity lemma gives

\[
M_r<M_{r+1}.
\]

Therefore

\[
\Gamma_r<\Gamma_{r+1}.
\]

Hence the sequence

\[
(\Gamma_r)_{r\ge 3}
\]

is strictly increasing. \(\blacksquare\)
